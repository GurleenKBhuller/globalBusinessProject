South Korea is politically favourable for business as it has various Free Trade Agreements such as the U.S.-Korea Free Trade Agreement (KORUS FTA) that reduce tariffs and trade barriers.

POLITICAL CHALLENGES

  • - Politics dominated by government and an opposition party, both having approval rates by public below 35%.
  • - South Korean President Yoon Suk-yeol has shifted the country's foreign policy toward the United States (Pro-US Foreign Policy) but hasn't seen a significant increase in his domestic approval ratings
  • - Corruption among leaders, examples including manipulation of stock prices by them.

South Korea's status as the 10th largest economy in the world and the 3rd largest in Asia reflects its significant economic power and influence on the global stage.

ECONOMIC CHALLENGES

  • - Rapid economic development has led to an increase in the cost of property. Property prices are unrealistic, and property debt levels are excessive.
  • - Price pressure from domestic manufacturers would be pronounced as Incredible eats is a foreign company

South Korea is appealing for Incredible Eats as the nation embraces a zero-food-waste and plastic-free culture as individuals increasingly prioritize sustainable consumption and lifestyles.

SOCIAL CHALLENGES

  • - Language barrier in South Korea can be challenging, as the majority of the population primarily speaks Korean.
  • - Behavorial differences (e.g., In USA, direct eye contact conveys confidence and respect, but in Korea, it can be viewed as confrontational and impolite.), accents, food and lifestyle differences are prevalent.
  • - Due to low birth rate and long life expectancy, about half of South Korea's population is above 65 years; which means shortage of young labor.

South Korea is known for its innovation in robotics, artificial intelligence, and green technologies, positioning it as a prominent player in the tech industry on the global stage.

TECHNICAL CHALLENGE

  • - The dominance of social media and the highly competitive online presence in South Korea present significant challenges to the popularity of the company in the market.

The South Korean Ministry of Food and Drug Safety (MFDS) enforces stringent regulations and rigorous inspections to ensure food safety, including monitoring labeling, hazard analysis, and international cooperation.

LEGAL CHALLENGE

  • - Korean labor market practices differ significantly from the American "at-will" employment system. In South Korea, employers are required to demonstrate "just cause" for terminating employment, making the process more stringent and structured.

South Korea, one of the world's top consumers of single-use plastic packaging, implemented a ban on all single-use plastic bags at major supermarkets starting in January, 2019, as part of its ambitious goals to reduce plastic waste to 50%, and increase recycling rates from the current to 70% by 2030. This aligns with the goals of Incredible Eats too.

ENVIRONMENTAL CHALLENGE

  • - South Korea have water scarcity and pollution issues, and climate change is making the problem even worse.
