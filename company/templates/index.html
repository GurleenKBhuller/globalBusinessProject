Across the world, a staggering amount of people are eating their food with single-use plastic utensils but why do we use something just for few minutes if it's gonna take hundreds of years to decompose? It's a moment on the lips but a lifetime in a landfill. That's why, IncrEDIBLE Eats was founded to create the world's first mass-manufacturable and entirely edible cutlery, offering an incredible solution.



1
2007

Dinesh Tadepalli, an engineer originally from Guntur, Andhra Pradesh, South India, moves to the U.S. to pursue his Master's degree after completing his undergraduate studies in Electrical Engineering in Chennai, Tamil Nadu.

2018

While having ice cream with his daughter at a dessert parlor, Tadepalli is shocked to see a bin filled with a massive amount of plastic spoons. This incident triggers his commitment to reducing plastic pollution.

2
3
2019

Tadepalli launches his mission to find a sustainable alternative to single-use plastic cutlery. He discovers a Hyderabad-based startup selling edible spoons but decides to set up his own production machinery in the U.S.

February 2019

The first edible spoons made from non-GMO corn, wheat, chickpeas, and brown rice are introduced to the world under the company's original name, "Planeteer.

4
5
2019 Later

In 2019, the company initially targeted B2B food service as the primary market for their edible cutlery due to the high use of plastic in this sector. However, the COVID-19 pandemic led the company to explore B2C and direct-to-consumer sales as well.

2021

IncrEDIBLE Eats expands its product line beyond spoons, with the introduction of edible sporks and straws.

6
7
Later 2021

Tadepalli and IncrEDIBLE Eats make a successful appearance on Shark Tank's Season 13, impressing the sharks with his vision of replacing plastic cutlery to save the planet. He secures an investment deal with Lori Greiner.

Post Shark Tank Success

After the Shark Tank episode airs, IncrEDIBLE Eats receives pre-orders of $50,000 in just two days, boosting the company's success.

8
9
2022

The company partners with Dippin' Dots, an iconic beaded ice cream company, to offer its edible cutlery as a sustainable alternative.

2023

The company is valued at around $2 million, and they've opened a 2023 SAFE note offering to expand their pool of investors. They have sold over 6 million spoons to date and are expanding their product offerings, including different shapes and flavors.
