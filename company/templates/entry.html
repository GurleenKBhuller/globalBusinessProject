ENTRY STRATEGY is EXPORTING first and then later on WHOLLY OWNED SUBSIDIARY.
The company's initial market entry strategy is centered around exports. Our partner, Dippin' Dots will serve as the vehicle through which the company's products will be distributed and sold in the target market. Dippin' Dots is selling through about 2000 Conveneince stores such as 7 Eleven, Buy the Way, etc., 490 Supermarkets, some of which are Lotte Super, E-Mart, GS Super, etc; and 120 local retailers and restaurants.
This initial phase of market entry through export will serve as a means of testing the waters and gaining a foothold in the new market. It will allows Incredible Eats to leverage Dippin' Dots' existing distribution network and brand recognition to make the products available to a wide range of potential customers.
The company's long-term strategy involves evolving beyond this export-oriented approach. In the future, we intend to transition to a "Wholly Owned Subsidiary" model. The company plans to establish its own presence and infrastructure within the target market after taking and working on the feedback of customers and customizing according to the local flavors.
Transnational Strategy involves adapting the edible spoons, chopsticks and forks to South Korean tastes, while maintaining a consistent global brand. The taste needs to establish a local presence, so the products will be customized for the South Korean market, and the universal messaging will be combined with local flavors. This approach allows for global recognition while addressing the specific needs and cultural nuances of the South Korean market.
At first, Incredible Eats will be sending out its products which are customized according to South Korean flavors found out through market research. Then, on getting user feedback and with the help of partners in South Korea, such as Dippin' Dots, the company will keep customizing. Once Incredible Eats switches from Export to Wholly Owned Subsidiary model, it will employ a mix of USA and South Korean workers to help cater to locals there.
ENTRY STRUCTURE chosen by Incredible Eats is TRANSNATIONAL.

MARKETING

Incredible Eats will tap into South Korea's eco-conscious culture, promoting edible cutlery as an eco-friendly and health-conscious alternative to plastics, and will seek support from local influencer collaborations and digital marketing.

  1. - The core product, edible cutlery will remain same.
    - Edible spoons, forks and chopsticks, and fully biodegradable packaging is the concept and USP of Incredible Eats.
    - South Koreans have different taste and flavor preferences than US citizens, so new flavors and variations of cutlery that are more appealing to the South Korean palate will be introduced.
    - Spoons are a major portion of product range in USA, which will be switched to Chopsticks as being the major portion.
  2. - The value of prices of spoons and chopsticks ranges from a few cents to a few thousand dollars in Korean market, depending on the size, style and look.
    - Edible cutlery is a favorable concept for South Korea and there are no major brands in this area.
    - Since South Koreans are very concerned about environment and health, they will prefer eatable cutlery over plastic, and hence are willing to pay.
    - Also, low-price strategy is also not perceived to have additional value in the minds of Korean consumers (Walmart's case). So, the prices will be same.
    - These will later be changed after understanding consumer buying habits, frequency and feedback.
  3. - The brand's core values, mission, and USP will remain consistent.
    - The cutlery will be customized into a new design and layout each year to keep up with the changing attitudes of Korean consumers.
    - South Koreans are known to appreciate high-quality brands and are easily influenced by social media, and popular culture. So, platforms like Naver, KakaoTalk, and Instagram will be used for marketing digitally.
    - Incredible Eats will collaborate with local influencers or celebrities who can help represent the brand as something familiar and promote the products to South Korean audience.
    - The company will participate in local and government events and festivals that align with our no-plastic brand values where we will sample our products.
  4. - Incredible Eats will create and maintain a strong online presence, with our own website's Korean version for South Korean market.
    - The partnership with Dippin' Dots will be helpful in initial distribution of the product to the customers, and later on, the company will establish more local partnerships.
    - Online commerce websites such as coupang.com, e-himart.co.kr, ssg.com, hmall.com, and gmarket.co.kr will be utilized to sell the products directly to consumers.

DISTRIBUTION AND SUPPLY CHAIN

- Initially, products will be exported from the USA itself, where a fully- fleged manufacturing plant and warehouses are set up. For Distribution, the company is going to use the distribution network of Dippin' Dots, its partner in South Korea.
- Later on, after understanding the details of Korean market and gaining consumer base, the company will shift to South Korea and set up a plant there.
- Clear study is being conducted on market trends and consumer preferences in South Korea. Since there is lesser initial idea on demand of product in South Korea, some extra inventory will be kept to meet some unexpected fluctuation in demand of products.
- Inventory will also be kept in South Korea for continuing distribution even in the face of disruptions or delays from USA.

TECHNICAL

- The cutlery will be redesigned to align with Korean preferences. Engineering processes will be adapted to meet local regulations, standards, and market demands in South Korea. This may involve redesigning products or processes to align with local requirements.
- Innovations will be brought regularly and frequently when the company keeps learning from South Korea. The Korean tech practices will be adopted.
- Even though thr immediate distribution will be done through export strategy, local suppliers and technology providers will be identified and relationships will be established with them.
- Our technology already follows a negative plastic and negative carbon approach, which is the standard with technical requirements in South Korea.

HR Policies in USA vs South Korea
  1. - ORGANIZATIONAL COMMUNICATION:
    Formal, top-down communication, with superiors giving general directives and subordinates interpreting them, leads to misunderstandings and hinders open expression of opposing views in formal meetings.
    - ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE:
    Companies in South Korea have tall hierarchical structure with decision-making authority concentrated at senior management levels and horizontal functional control in staff departments.
    - LEADERSHIP:
    Leadership style is directive and authoritarian influenced by family and clan systems.
    -RECRUITING:
    Managers are often recruited from elite backgrounds and form power groups through geographic and school connections. Friends from hometowns or universities are given preference while hiring.
    -PERFORMANCE APPRAISAL:
    Appraisals are based on seniority and performance. Wages are usually decided by seniority level and bonese are judged by performance.
  2. - ORGANIZATIONAL COMMUNICATION:
    American companies combine formal and informal communication and use different network structures based on organizational strategy and structure.
    - ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE:
    Companies in USA have various organizational styles based on company size, technology and environmental certainity.

    -LEADERSHIP:
    Leadership style in USA is more diverse and participative with minimal political connections.
    -RECRUITING:
    U.S. laws and regulations governing recruitment and hiring mandate decisions free from discrimination based on race, sex, religion, age, or disability.
    -PERFORMANCE APPRAISAL:
    USA believes that the strong will survive and the weak will perish. Rewards and promotions are based on individual's success or failures, not a group.

Korean management strategies are adopted from Japnese management policies but is now moving towards direct American HRM techniques. Even though South Korea has a very different culture than USA, since Koreans are exposed to globalization or overseas culture traits, they find it easy to work in a diverse culture.
The Hybrid HR approach chosen by Incredible Eats combines elements from both countries to create a harmonious and effective HR strategy.
After Hiring some South Korean candidates,
TRAINING: Cultural sensitivity training will be provided to all employees, from both countries, to develop understanding and respect for each other's work styles.
COMMUNICATION: South Korean employees will be encouraged to give their views and participate in discussions without hesitation.
LEGAL: New HR policies and practices will comply with the labor laws and regulations of both South Korea and the USA.
WAGES: It will be made sure that the wages for workers immigrating from South Korea to USA in the first phase of company shift export policy are provided wages that comply with South Korean standards.
ADDITIONAL BENEFITS: Appraisals, bonuses and vacations, etc. will be provided for motivation of employees.
