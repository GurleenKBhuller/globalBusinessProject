South Korea is a society that accepts hierarchy, favors centralized authority, and often values benevolent autocratic leadership.

South Korea represents a highly collectivistic society, emphasizing strong, long-term commitments to family and group relationships, where loyalty and responsibility to the group are paramount, and social rules often take precedence over individual interests.

South Korea is tilted towards feminine qualities and is a Consensus society that emphasizes equality, solidarity, and well-being, with managers striving for consensus and decisions made through involvement and negotiation.

South Korea, with a high Uncertainty Avoidance score, is characterized by a culture that values strict codes of behavior, resists unorthodox ideas, prioritizes precision, punctuality, and hard work, and places significant importance on security in individual motivation.

South Korea is among the most pragmatic and long-term-oriented societies, emphasizing virtues, practical examples, and the durability of companies serving stakeholders and society over generations.

South Korea's low Indulgence score indicates a cultural tendency toward cynicism and pessimism, a lesser emphasis on leisure, and a belief that indulgence goes against social norms.

POWER DISTANCE
While taking the company from USA to South Korea, there is a shift from an egalitarian, participative work culture to a more hierarchical, centralized one. So, building relationships with officials, respecting local customs and adapting to leadership style will be crucial.
INDIVIDUALISM
Adapting to the shift from individualism in USA to collectivism in South Korea, where group loyalty and social rules are paramount, is going to need adjustments in decision-making processes and management styles. The company will foster strong interpersonal relationships, consider group dynamics in decision-making, and prioritize long-term commitment to build trust and succeed in the South Korean market.
UNCERTAINITY AVOIDANCE
This factor might be an indication of the challenges related to divergent attitudes toward innovation and risk-taking. To navigate this, Incredible Eats will prioritize clear communication, and demonstrate a respect for established norms and protocols.
MASCULINE VS FEMININE
When taking Incredible Eats from the US (a masculine society) to South Korea (a consensus-based, feminine society), challenges may arise concerning decision-making styles and priorities. To overcome this, the company will emphasize on feminine values such as consensus-building, and a more collaborative work culture that values both individual success and group cohesion.
SHORT TERM VS LONG TERM PERSPECTIVE
There are differing cultural orientations towards practicality and long-term focus between USA and South Korea. To address this, the company will ensure that the business strategies align with South Korea's emphasis on enduring value, relationships, and the long-term well-being of stakeholders and society such as by prioritizing to steady growth of market share rather than to a quarterly profit.
INDULGENCE
Indulgence index goes down while moving from USA to South Korea. It indicates the potential challenge of differences in work-life balance and social norms. To succeed in the market, Incredible Eats will customize products taking into account the local values, promote a more balanced work environment, and ensure that the business practices align with South Korean cultural expectations regarding restraint and social norms.
